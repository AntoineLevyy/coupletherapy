# HappyCouple Moment Flow Product Spec

## Goal

Reposition HappyCouple around immediate relationship value: help users handle their relationship better, one concrete moment at a time, with useful guidance in under 2 minutes.

## Product thesis

Users should not have to commit to a long session or report before feeling value. The first experience should let them bring one real relationship problem, then receive prescriptive guidance: what may be underneath, what to say or do next, and how to avoid making it worse.

## Positioning

Headline:
Handle your relationship better, one moment at a time.

Subheadline:
Tell HappyCouple what is going on. Get clarity on what is underneath, what to say next, and how to move forward — in under 2 minutes.

Primary prompt:
How can I help your relationship today?

CTA:
Get help with this moment

## Suggested problem examples

Show visible prompt chips under the hero input:
- We keep having the same fight
- I need to bring up something hard
- I want to apologize better
- I feel disconnected from my partner
- I am not sure if I am overreacting
- My partner gets defensive
- We need to talk about money
- We are struggling with intimacy
- I want us to feel close again

## First-use flow

1. User lands on homepage.
2. User types a concrete relationship situation or clicks a suggested prompt.
3. User proceeds through required safety/not-therapy consent.
4. User chooses whether they are doing this solo or together.
5. Voice session starts with the user situation already in context.
6. Agent immediately narrows the broad request into a concrete coaching track.

## Agent behavior

The agent must be prescriptive, not generic chat.

Opening behavior:
- Acknowledge the user's situation.
- Say the goal is to make the situation concrete.
- Ask for one specific recent moment if the user was vague.
- If concrete enough, ask at most one clarifying question.
- Then guide toward three outputs:
  1. what may be underneath,
  2. what to say or do next,
  3. what to avoid so it does not escalate.

Agent must not ask, “What would you like to do?” too early. It should say, “Here is how we will approach this.”

## Output promise

For a short first session, the user should leave with:
- a plain-language read of the underlying pattern,
- one practical next move,
- one better sentence or conversation opener,
- one thing to avoid,
- one optional deeper path.

## Deeper conversion paths

After immediate value, offer one next step based on the situation:
- Practice the conversation.
- Invite partner to share their side.
- Save this as a recurring pattern.
- Do a deeper relationship check-in.
- Create a small plan for the next week.

## Non-goals for MVP

- Do not lead with a 15-minute session.
- Do not lead with a full report.
- Do not position as therapy.
- Do not make it a generic relationship chatbot.
- Do not require account creation before first value.

## Success criteria

A new visitor can understand the value in 5 seconds, enter one relationship moment, and start a guided session that feels specific within the first 30 seconds.
